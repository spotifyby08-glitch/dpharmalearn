Preparation, properties, and evaluation of pharmaceutical solutions including syrups, elixirs, and aromatic waters.

Key Topics: Solvents, solubility, syrups, elixirs, spirits, aromatic waters, monophasic liquid dosage forms.

Suspensions are biphasic systems where a solid is dispersed in a liquid medium. This chapter covers formulation and stability considerations.

Key Topics: Flocculated vs. deflocculated suspensions, sedimentation, suspending agents, evaluation parameters.

Emulsions are two-phase systems used widely in oral and topical products. Learn about types, emulsifying agents, and stability.

Key Topics: O/W and W/O emulsions, HLB system, emulsifying agents, emulsification methods, evaluation.

Semisolid dosage forms used topically. This chapter details formulation, bases, and evaluation of creams, ointments, gels, and pastes.

Key Topics: Ointment bases (hydrocarbon, water-soluble), creams, gels, pastes, preservatives, packaging.

Pharmaceutical powders are one of the simplest dosage forms. This chapter covers preparation, size reduction, and granulation techniques.

Key Topics: Size reduction, comminution, sieving, blending, granulation methods — wet and dry, dustiness issues.

Tablets are the most popular oral dosage form. This chapter covers types, excipients, manufacturing methods, and quality control.

Key Topics: Tablet types, diluents, binders, disintegrants, lubricants, tablet coating, quality control tests.

Hard and soft gelatin capsules — their preparation, filling, and quality evaluation.

Key Topics: Hard gelatin capsules (HGC), soft gelatin capsules (SGC), capsule filling, evaluation, storage conditions.
