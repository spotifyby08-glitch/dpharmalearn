Pharmaceutical Chemistry I

Introduction to pharmaceutical chemistry, types of errors in analysis, sources and classification of impurities in drug substances, and limit tests overview.

Key Topics: Scope of pharmaceutical chemistry, determinate & indeterminate errors, accuracy vs precision, organic/inorganic/residual solvent impurities, ICH Q3A/Q3B, limit test principles.

Read Full Notes Download PDF

Official IP pharmacopoeial limit tests for inorganic impurities. Covers principle, reagents, procedure, and precautions for each test.

Key Topics: AgNO₃ test for chlorides, BaCl₂ test for sulphates, thioglycolic acid test for iron, H₂S/thioacetamide test for heavy metals, Gutzeit method for arsenic.

Read Full Notes Download PDF

Iron preparations used to treat anaemia and essential electrolyte drugs — their properties, uses, doses, and IP tests.

Key Topics: Ferrous sulphate, ferrous gluconate, ferrous fumarate; NaCl, NaHCO₃, KCl; WHO-ORS composition; iron absorption factors.

Read Full Notes Download PDF

Inorganic drugs acting on the GI tract — antacids that neutralise gastric acid, laxatives that promote bowel movement, and adsorbents used in poisoning.

Key Topics: NaHCO₃, Al(OH)₃, Mg(OH)₂, Mg trisilicate, CaCO₃; MgSO₄ (Epsom salt), Na₂SO₄ (Glauber's salt), liquid paraffin; activated charcoal, kaolin.

Read Full Notes Download PDF

Inorganic drugs applied locally to skin and mucous membranes, agents used in dentistry, and pharmaceutical-grade gases with medical uses.

Key Topics: ZnO, H₂O₂, KMnO₄, boric acid, AgNO₃; NaF (dental caries), ZnCl₂, Ca(OH)₂; O₂, N₂O, CO₂ — cylinder colours and uses.

Read Full Notes Download PDF

Foundational organic chemistry concepts essential for understanding drug molecules — hybridization, IUPAC naming, functional groups, and isomerism.

Key Topics: sp³/sp²/sp hybridization; IUPAC nomenclature rules; functional groups (–OH, –COOH, –NH₂, ester, amide, nitro); structural and stereoisomerism; thalidomide tragedy; aspirin synthesis.

Read Full Notes Download PDF

Chemistry and pharmacology of sulphonamide antibacterials and major antibiotic classes — their structures, mechanisms, uses, and adverse effects.

Key Topics: Sulphonamide structure-activity relationship, sulphadiazine, cotrimoxazole; penicillins, cephalosporins, tetracyclines, chloramphenicol.

Read Full Notes Download PDF

Chemistry and pharmacology of drugs used for pain relief — from aspirin and paracetamol to opioid analgesics.

Key Topics: Aspirin (synthesis, SAR, adverse effects), paracetamol, ibuprofen, diclofenac; opioid analgesics — morphine, codeine, pethidine; COX inhibition mechanism.

Read Full Notes Download PDF

Drugs acting on the cardiovascular and central nervous systems — their chemical structures, mechanisms of action, and therapeutic uses.

Key Topics: Antihypertensives, cardiac glycosides, diuretics; sedatives, hypnotics, anticonvulsants, antidepressants; structure-activity relationships.

Read Full Notes Download PDF

Chemistry, deficiency diseases, sources, and therapeutic uses of vitamins and hormones essential for body function.

Key Topics: Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) and water-soluble vitamins (B-complex, C); insulin, glucagon, thyroid hormones, corticosteroids; deficiency diseases.
